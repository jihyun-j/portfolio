Overview

I had an opportunity that works as a UX design apprentice at BRIDGEGOOD. BRIDGEGOOD is a non-profit organization that supports design students develop their skills and industry knowledge. My teammates and I were assigned a design sprint to create the internship/entry-level job opportunities feature to provide inclusive and accessible job opportunities for entry-level design students. During work with my tames, I had a chance to do user interviews and finalized high-fidelity wireframes.

Problem

Job hunting is not easy for students since they lack experience. Besides, most companies prefer applicants with more than two years of experience, and they post many requirements on the job posting. It makes students overwhelmed and hesitant to apply. Students from the BRIDGEGOOD UX Design Apprenticeship have limited-to-no industry experience and want to gain real-world experience by getting hired for an internship.

Design Challenge

Designing an Internship/Entry Level Job Opportunities feature on BRIDGEGOOD.com, where companies can post available internship positions for students to apply to. BRIDGEGOOD can receive referral fees by connecting qualified students to companies.

Competitor Audit

I had a responsibility to analyze the competitors. We chose the most popular job posting platforms, and I took charge of researching those job platforms. I quickly tested each platform, analyzed its key features, and used secondary research contexts.

Strength

  • Social media platform
  • Simple to look for job and filtering
  • The most popular job searching platform
  • Strong content and feartures for job seeker

Weakness

  • May create a unverified or fake profile
  • Privacy
  • Premium membership
  • Intense competition (e.g. Facebook)

Strength

  • University partnership
  • Partnership with out of state university (UK)
  • Exclusive career fair

Weakness

  • Lack of community college partnership
  • Not every major university or college is on the platform
  • Platform fee

Strength

  • Helping start-ups get connect to both investor and employees
  • More visualized job description (Company Photos & Videos)
  • Using keywords on job description

Weakness

  • Lack of contents for job seeker
  • Confuse to navigate website
  • May create a unverified or fake profile

The Solution

Landing Page

Giving trustworthy and BRIDGEGOOD values.

Showing BRIDGEGOOD Appretices program and their qualification to build partnership with companies.

Company - Validation Form

Partnership with suitable companies.

Confirm the companies relate to design or tech industry so that the student can work on right position that they want.

Student - Job Searching / View Postings

Easy to searching and developing confidence.

Students can search by their specialties, status, type of role and location and giving a comments to encourage to apply.

Student - Job Application Form

Non-traditional Application

Get rid of eduction and cover letter sections to make students less overwhelmed.

BRIDGEGOOD & Student - Application Tracker

Tracking Job Application

The BRIDGEGOOD can keep track on hired students and provide referral. Also, student can request referral to BRIDGEGOOD and track their application status.

Reflection

  1. It is natural to have new assumptions while building new products. And it is crucial to validate the assumption with users.
  2. It is okay to go back to the previous phase while developing products. It gives me the possibility to find uncovered facts. The iteration of all stages allowed me to create a better solution.
  3. It provides a chance to deal with multiple users. This project has to satisfy various perspectives since the target audiences were students, companies, and stakeholders. I had an opportunity to find the middle point to give them the confidence to use it.
  4. It is important to focus on accessibility and usability but also needs to understand the business goals and values.
